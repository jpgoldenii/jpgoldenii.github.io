Megalodon Shark Terrorizes Bear Lake

By Jim Golden

A large creature resembling a shark with an estimated length of 50 feet, has been recorded by a drone flying over the northern end of Bear Lake last month. Sadie Hawkins, the pilot of the drone, said that she was surprised and frightened at what she was witnessing on her monitor. “I thought at first it was just a shadow, but then it began to move around – circling the boats like it was stalking its prey”.

Even though sharks have been spotted in freshwater rivers, Scientist and Marine Biologist dispute the notion that a shark of that size could be in such a small lake. Albert Cousteau, 4th-cousin to the famous Jacques Cousteau, said; “I could see a Megalodon living in Lake Superior, but not Bear Lake.”

Boaters and kayakers are flocking to the southern Idaho lake with hopes of getting a glimpse of the mammoth shark. Buford T. Justice told the Preston Star, “I wouldn’t mind if that shark took a big bite out of my Jon boat. I could make a lot selling it on eBay.”
