Crowned King of Corona

By Jim Golden

Temperature are on the rise. As thousands of people waited in line for hours to be tested for COVID-19 in the blistering heat on Wednesday, their level of frustration was increasing as well.

“With the number of COVID-19 cases on the rise, testing has become a big issue,” says Deputy Director Sars Ebola, with the Division of Emergency Management, which oversees the testing site.

“The line of cars stretched all the way back to Billy’s Hardware to get a test” Preston resident Marcel Ledbetter said. “I was sure I going to run out of gas before I got my turn.” With temperature nearing 95 degrees in the afternoon sun, people were beginning to lose patience. “I was so mad hot, they were thinking I had the Corona. They started hollering out to the folks in the hazmat suits to come over and quarantine me.” Marcel said.
